Subtheme: Visualizing Exclusionary Zoning

Overall Analysis Questions

  1. How has the “executive belt” surrounding Boston changed? Do these areas still have extensive single-family zoning?
  2. Are there certain municipalities that have higher fractions of non-white residents? Do municipalities with higher fractions have less single-family zoning? Are there observable differences in income, household size, and number of children?
  3. Which municipalities that sought “no growth” achieved it? How much did they grow/evolve since the 1970's?
  4. How are rail network access and municipal housing composition related?

Discoveries & Insights

The dataset provided by The Warren Group consists of information on residential zoning, demographics, and household income and composition for municipalities served by the Boston Metropolitian Area Planning Council. Prior to engaging with this data, I read Exclusionary by Design by Amy Dain. This report details the history of exclusionary zoning practices in Greater Boston, including fiscal, class, and racist zoning, since the 1920's. My analysis will focus on similar themes. Before approaching my overall analysis questions, I began with a brief review of data quality. The first thing I discovered was that some municipalities are duplicated in the dataset. For such municipalities, the data on areas zoned for single-family housing is recorded separately and notated by a only_single_housing flag.
